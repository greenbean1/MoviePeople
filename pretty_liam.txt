     <p>
      <b>
       Liam John Neeson
      </b>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Officer of the Order of the British Empire">
       OBE
      </a>
      (born 7 June 1952)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swe_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-swe-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">
       Irish
      </a>
      actor. In 1976, he joined the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyric_Players%27_Theatre" title="Lyric Players' Theatre">
       Lyric Players' Theatre
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">
       Belfast
      </a>
      for two years. He then acted in the
      <a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">
       Arthurian
      </a>
      film,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Excalibur_(film)" title="Excalibur (film)">
        Excalibur
       </a>
      </i>
      (1981). Between 1982 and 1987, Neeson starred in five films; most notably alongside
      <a href="/wiki/Mel_Gibson" title="Mel Gibson">
       Mel Gibson
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hopkins" title="Anthony Hopkins">
       Anthony Hopkins
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Bounty" title="The Bounty">
        The Bounty
       </a>
      </i>
      (1984) and
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_De_Niro" title="Robert De Niro">
       Robert De Niro
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Irons" title="Jeremy Irons">
       Jeremy Irons
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Mission_(1986_film)" title="The Mission (1986 film)">
        The Mission
       </a>
      </i>
      (1986). He landed a leading role alongside
      <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Swayze" title="Patrick Swayze">
       Patrick Swayze
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Next_of_Kin_(1989_film)" title="Next of Kin (1989 film)">
       <i>
        Next of Kin
       </i>
      </a>
      (1989).
     </p>
     <p>
      He rose to prominence when he starred in the title role in
      <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">
       Steven Spielberg
      </a>
      's 1993
      <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Picture" title="Academy Award for Best Picture">
       Oscar
      </a>
      winner
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Schindler%27s_List" title="Schindler's List">
        Schindler's List
       </a>
      </i>
      . He has since starred in other successful films, including the title role in the historical biopic
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(film)" title="Michael Collins (film)">
        Michael Collins
       </a>
      </i>
      (1996), the
      <a href="/wiki/Film_adaptation" title="Film adaptation">
       film adaptation
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Victor_Hugo" title="Victor Hugo">
       Victor Hugo
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/Les_Mis%C3%A9rables" title="Les Misérables">
       1862 novel
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Les_Mis%C3%A9rables_(1998_film)" title="Les Misérables (1998 film)">
        Les Misérables
       </a>
      </i>
      (1998), the epic space opera
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_I:_The_Phantom_Menace" title="Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace">
        Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace
       </a>
      </i>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn" title="Qui-Gon Jinn">
       Qui-Gon Jinn
      </a>
      (1999), the biographical drama
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Kinsey_(film)" title="Kinsey (film)">
        Kinsey
       </a>
      </i>
      (2004), the superhero film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Batman_Begins" title="Batman Begins">
        Batman Begins
       </a>
      </i>
      as
      <a href="/wiki/Ra%27s_al_Ghul" title="Ra's al Ghul">
       Ra's al Ghul
      </a>
      (2005), the action thriller series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Taken_(film_series)" title="Taken (film series)">
        Taken
       </a>
      </i>
      (2008–14), the fantasy adventure film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Clash_of_the_Titans_(2010_film)" title="Clash of the Titans (2010 film)">
        Clash of the Titans
       </a>
      </i>
      (2010), the fantasy films in the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia_(film_series)" title="The Chronicles of Narnia (film series)">
        The Chronicles of Narnia
       </a>
      </i>
      series (2005–10), and the thriller-survival film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Grey_(film)" title="The Grey (film)">
        The Grey
       </a>
      </i>
      (2011).
     </p>
     <p>
      He has been nominated for a number of awards, including an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">
       Academy Award for Best Actor
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_of_Film_and_Television_Arts" title="British Academy of Film and Television Arts">
       BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Leading Role
      </a>
      and three
      <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actor_%E2%80%93_Motion_Picture_Drama" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Drama">
       Golden Globe Awards for Best Actor in a Motion Picture Drama
      </a>
      .
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Empire_(film_magazine)" title="Empire (film magazine)">
        Empire
       </a>
      </i>
      magazine ranked Neeson among both the "100 Sexiest Stars in Film History" and "The Top 100 Movie Stars of All Time".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">
       Early life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

     <p>
      Neeson was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Ballymena" title="Ballymena">
       Ballymena
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/County_Antrim" title="County Antrim">
       County Antrim
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">
       Northern Ireland
      </a>
      , the son of Katherine "Kitty" Neeson (née Brown), a cook, and Bernard "Barney" Neeson, a
      <a href="/wiki/Property_caretaker" title="Property caretaker">
       caretaker
      </a>
      at the Ballymena Boys All Saints Primary School.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He was raised as a Roman Catholic
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and was named Liam after the local priest.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The third of four siblings, he has three sisters: Elizabeth, Bernadette, and Rosaleen.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At age nine, Neeson began
      <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">
       boxing

     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/Truck_driver" title="Truck driver">

      <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyric_Players%27_Theatre" title="Lyric Players' Theatre">

      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilgrim%27s_Progress" title="Pilgrim's Progress">
        Pilgrim's Progress
       </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">
       Dublin
      </a>

      <i>
       Says I, Says He
      </i>
      , a drama about
      <a href="/wiki/The_Troubles" title="The Troubles">
       The Troubles
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Project_Arts_Centre" title="Project Arts Centre">
       Project Arts Centre
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Theatre" title="Abbey Theatre">
       Abbey Theatre
      </a>
      (the National Theatre of Ireland).

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Political_views">

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">

     <p>

       US gun laws
      </a>
      a "disgrace" in an interview with Dubai newspaper
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_News" title="Gulf News">
        Gulf News
       </a>
      </i>
      when replying to a question about the
      <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Hebdo_shooting" title="Charlie Hebdo shooting">
       <i>
        Charlie Hebdo
       </i>
       shootings
      </a>
      in Paris earlier that month.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_News_21_January_2015_59-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC_News_21_January_2015-59">
        <span>
         [
        </span>

        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Para_USA" title="Para USA">
       Para USA
      </a>

      <i>
       Taken
      </i>
      film series, expressed regret at working with him, saying: "We will no longer provide firearms for use in films starring Liam Neeson and ask that our friends and partners in Hollywood refrain from associating our brand and products with his projects".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_News_21_January_2015_59-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC_News_21_January_2015-59">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        59
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a href="/wiki/Bill_de_Blasio" title="Bill de Blasio">
       Bill de Blasio
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Central_Park" title="Central Park">
       Central Park
      </a>

      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">
        The New York Times
       </a>
